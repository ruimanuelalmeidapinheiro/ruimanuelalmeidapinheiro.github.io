09 July 2026 · The start of the Measurement Problem series.
The measurement problem is not merely a technical complication in quantum mechanics; it is the fundamental threshold where human observation encounters the limits of classical description. It represents the persistent tension between the unitary, deterministic evolution of the wave function and the singular, definite reality we perceive upon observation. This new series initiates a rigorous exploration of this transition, moving beyond the superficial "billiard ball" interpretations that characterize mainstream discourse. We will examine why the collapse of the wave function serves as a crucial residue of our descriptive framework rather than a physical process to be "solved." By mapping the boundaries of objectivity, locality, and separability, we aim to recontextualize measurement as a consequence of our perceptual architecture. This series serves to illuminate the gap between the mathematical elegance of quantum foundations and the interface we mistake for existence itself.
