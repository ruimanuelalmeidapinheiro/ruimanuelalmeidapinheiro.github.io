Formulating local evolutionary complexity as the field's optimized mechanism for phase de-coherence and spectrum dissipation.

Within this paradigm, the spontaneous emergence of complex structures—such as biological life, neurological consciousness, and technological networks—presents a strange paradox. These systems seem to rebel against the dominant thermodynamic slide by creating local islands of order.

When analyzed through a pure frequency framework, this apparent paradox disappears. Ilya Prigogine's insights into non-equilibrium systems demonstrate that local complexity is not a rebellion against entropy; it is its primary acceleration engine. The universe does not select for static order.
