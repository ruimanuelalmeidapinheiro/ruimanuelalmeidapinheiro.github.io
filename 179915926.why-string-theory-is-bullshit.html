Why String Theory is Bullshit

This article delivers a deterministic critique of String Theory, labeling it "bullshit" not for mathematical ugliness, but for empirical emptiness. It argues the theory has failed for 50 years to produce a single falsifiable prediction, instead retreating into a $10^{500}$-fold "landscape" of compactifications that can fit any data, explaining nothing. With the LHC finding no supersymmetry and goalposts constantly shifting, the piece condemns the field's institutional monoculture for stifling rival approaches (like Loop Quantum Gravity) while redefining science to prioritize elegance over evidence. Citing founders like Susskind and Gross who admit its disconnect from reality, the author frames String Theory as a degenerate research program: a seductive, resource-draining narrative indistinguishable from unfalsifiable speculation, violating the core scientific mandate that theory must answer to experiment.

Here is a blunt, no-holds-barred summary of the main charges critics level against string theory. None of this means the math is *ugly*—it’s gorgeous—but “beautiful” is not the same as “true.” The case for calling string theory “bullshit” (in the technical, philosophical sense of “indistinguishable from unfalsifiable speculation”) boils down to six points:

1. **Zero contact with reality**
After five decades, the theory has not produced a single *definite*, *quantitative* prediction that experiments can actually check. Every time the energy scale moves up, the goal-posts shift again .



2. **The “landscape” swindle**
To hide the 6–7 extra spatial dimensions you have to “compactify” them. It turns out there are at least 10^500 ways to do this (the famous *landscape*). With that many knobs you can fit *any* low-energy physics you want, so the theory explains everything and therefore nothing .



3. **Supersymmetry—its only near-term hope—keeps not showing up**
The LHC has hammered large chunks of supersymmetry parameter space; so far, no super-partners. Every new null result is met with “Well, they must be heavier” rather than “Maybe the idea is wrong” .



4. **Institutional capture & opportunity cost**
Because string theory dominates hiring, grant panels and journal gate-keeping, rival approaches (loop quantum gravity, asymptotic safety, causal set theory, etc.) struggle to survive. The field has become a *monoculture*, not a marketplace of ideas .



5. **Moving the goal-posts on what counts as “science”**
When confronted with falsifiability, some gurus simply declare that Popper is old-fashioned and that we should accept *mathematical elegance* as evidence. Leonard Susskind even branded critics the “Popperazzi” . That rhetorical move insulates the theory from any conceivable experimental verdict.



6. **Even the founders are jumping ship**
Susskind now says flat-out: *“String theory is not the theory of the real world… I can tell you that 100 %”* . David Gross, who once called it the “only game in town,” admits *“We don’t know what string theory is”* any more .



Put together, the charge-sheet reads:
- No predictions →
- No falsifiability →
- No progress →
- Yet enormous resource lock-in.



That combination is why critics—from Peter Woit (*“Not Even Wrong”*) to Lee Smolin (*“The Trouble with Physics”*)—argue that string theory has mutated from a research program into a *degenerative project*: it consumes ever-more man-hours to produce ever-more baroque versions of the same untestable story .



So, if by “bullshit” you mean a seductive narrative that cannot be nailed down by empirical reality, string theory is—today—about as close as physics gets.
