Ideas in 2026

This article outlines ten visionary ideas for 2026: a paradigm shift in knowledge acquisition where discernment replaces censorship; individuals prioritizing personal moral systems over tradition; AI integrating as a collaborative cognitive partner; European identity rooted in shared Bell Beaker ancestry evolving into global citizenship; and the recognition that humanity—8.3 billion people—shares a unified quantum essence. The author anticipates breakthroughs in nuclear fusion enabling abundant clean energy, rendering scarcity-driven conflict obsolete. In this post-scarcity vision, money, banks, and deception fade, replaced by authentic communication and collective purpose. While acknowledging these ideas may seem premature, the piece concludes with humility: "I may be wrong," inviting dialogue rather than dogma.
