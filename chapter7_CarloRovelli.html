Profile: Carlo Rovelli

Carlo Rovelli is a world-renowned theoretical physicist, philosopher of science, and author known for his radical re-evaluations of the nature of space, time, and physical existence.

Current Academic Affiliations

  • Institution & Core Roles: Emeritus Professor at the Centre de Physique Théorique of Marseille (Aix-Marseille University), France.
  • Visiting & External Faculty positions: Distinguished Visiting Research Chair at the Perimeter Institute (Canada); Core Member of the Rotman Institute of Philosophy at Western University (Canada); Fractal Faculty Member at the Santa Fe Institute (USA).
  • Academies: Member of the Académie Internationale de Philosophie des Sciences and the Institut Universitaire de France.

Core Research Areas & Frameworks

Rovelli’s foundational work focuses on bridging the conceptual gaps between general relativity and quantum mechanics, advocating for a deeply relational and non-static architecture of reality.

  • Loop Quantum Gravity (LQG): Co-founder (alongside Lee Smolin and Abhay Ashtekar) of this leading background-independent candidate for quantum gravity. His work demonstrated the quantization of area and volume, establishing a discrete, non-continuous structure of space at the Planck scale.
  • Relational Quantum Mechanics (RQM): A framework asserting that the state of any physical system is not absolute, but always relative to another interacting system. It posits that reality is a web of relations rather than a collection of independent objects.
  • Physics Without Time: Development of a timeless classical and quantum mechanics formulation. Through the thermal time hypothesis (with Alain Connes), he argues that the flow of time is not a fundamental property of nature, but an emergent thermodynamic property arising from our macroscopic, incomplete state of knowledge.
  • Covariant Dynamics (Spin Foams): Introduced the "sum over surfaces" formulation and the spin foam vertex amplitude, creating the mathematical foundation for covariant loop quantum gravity dynamics.

Key Recent Literary & Philosophical Works

  • On the Equality of All Things (2026) – An exploration of relational reality, uncertainty, and circular knowledge, drawing parallels between modern physics and ancient Daoist philosophy.
  • 85 Seconds to Midnight (2026) – A searing historical and philosophical reckoning with short-sighted politics and the existential architecture of nuclear rearmament.
  • White Holes (2023) – A conceptual journey into what happens when a black hole reaches the end of its life, transforming into a white hole via quantum bounce.
  • Helgoland (2020) – The primary popular presentation of his Relational Quantum Mechanics framework.
