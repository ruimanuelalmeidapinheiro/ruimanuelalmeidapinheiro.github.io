Who is Erwin Schrödinger

Erwin Schrödinger (1887–1961): Austrian physicist. Nobel Prize in Physics (1933) for the Schrödinger equation, one of the foundational equations of quantum mechanics. Author of What is Life? (1944), a seminal work that explored the physical basis of life and inspired a generation of biologists, including Watson, Crick, and Delbrück. Schrödinger's work spanned quantum mechanics, thermodynamics, biology, and philosophy. He was a profound thinker who understood that the quantum nature of reality had deep implications for the nature of life.
