What is the Maximum Entropy Production Principle (MEPP)

The Maximum Entropy Production Principle (MEPP) is a thermodynamic principle stating that systems far from equilibrium will evolve toward states that maximize the rate of entropy production. It was developed by physicists and thermodynamicists including Ilya Prigogine, Rod Swenson, Eric Schneider, and James Kay.

Core thesis: The universe is not just moving toward equilibrium — it is doing so by the fastest possible route. When a system is driven away from equilibrium, it will self-organize to dissipate the imposed gradients as quickly as possible. This explains why complex structures emerge — they are the most efficient dissipation engines. Life, ecosystems, and even consciousness are all manifestations of this principle.
