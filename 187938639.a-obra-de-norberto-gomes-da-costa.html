📜 Extended Description of the Work

The work of Norberto Gomes da Costa is of great value for Portuguese Micro-History. His most significant contribution lies in two axes:

Axis 1: The Press as a Historical Source

The author does not merely list newspapers; he uses them as a lens to understand the social, political, and cultural life of Lafões between the late 19th century and the mid-20th century. His book Lafões: Imprensa e História is a fundamental reference that:

  • Identifies and Catalogs: Rescues from oblivion dozens of short-lived newspaper titles that would otherwise be lost.

  • Analyzes Discourse: Examines the way the press, often partisan (monarchist, republican, Catholic), shaped public opinion and local political struggles.

  • Documents the Political Transition: His studies on the Monarchy-Republic transition show, from opinion articles, how national ideals were perceived and experienced in small villages.

Axis 2: Preservation of the Regional Memory of Lafões

His focus on the Lafões region (Vouzela, Oliveira de Frades, S. Pedro do Sul) is a crucial contribution to historiography that focuses on less-studied geographical areas. His works help to reconstruct biographies of important local figures and to document the evolution of institutions (such as the Volunteer Firefighters or the Misericórdias) with documentary rigor.

In summary, the work of Norberto Gomes da Costa is a pillar of regional history, offering a meticulous and documentary-based study on the importance of social communication in the life of inland Portugal.

📝 Complete Bibliography of Norberto Gomes da Costa

The work of Norberto Gomes da Costa is divided into two major research themes: the Regional History and Press of Lafões and the Memory of the Portuguese Colonial War.

I. Works on Regional History and the Press (Lafões)

This is his area of greatest production as a historian, focused on the municipalities of Vouzela, Oliveira de Frades, and São Pedro do Sul. His works are primary sources for the study of republicanism, political journalism, and social life in inland Portugal.

Title Year of Publication Publisher/Context Detailed Description
Lafões: Imprensa e História (1895-1910) 2012 Nodar, S. Martinho das Moitas His fundamental monograph. This book analyzes the importance of the periodical press in the Lafões region during the final period of the Constitutional Monarchy, detailing the circulation of ideas, local political quarrels, and the atmosphere that preceded the Proclamation of the Republic in 1910.
O Notável Periódico "Terras de Lafões" e os seus Homens (1930-1941) c. 2013 Regional edition Investigation into one of the most influential newspapers in the region during the Military Dictatorship and the beginning of the Estado Novo. It examines the editorial board, the collaborators, and the way the periodical positioned itself vis-à-vis the central power and regional development issues.
A Imprensa Regional de Lafões na Primeira República 2014 Regional edition This work is a continuation of the 2012 book. It is dedicated to mapping and analyzing the local press during the political effervescence of the First Republic (1910–1926), highlighting ideological polarization and the involvement of newspapers in partisan struggles.
Vouzela, a Imprensa e o Fim da Monarquia e a Implantação da República c. 2014 Regional edition Focused specifically on the municipality of Vouzela, this book examines in detail the political transition from 1908 to 1910, using Vouzela newspapers as the main documentary source.
Artigos no Gazeta da Beira 2013 – Present Regional Periodical Norberto Gomes da Costa is a regular columnist and contributor to the Gazeta da Beira, where he publishes biweekly historical research chronicles. These articles delve into regional themes, such as the life and work of local historical figures (e.g., Dr. Ferreira de Almeida) and the dynamics of old newspapers (e.g., O Ecos do Vouga).

II. Works on the Portuguese Colonial War

Norberto Gomes da Costa is also a Member of the “Tabanca Grande” and an active contributor to the preservation of the memory of combatants in Guinea-Bissau, where he served as a Furriel (Sergeant) Miliciano Rifleman of Infantry.

Title Year of Publication Co-authorship/Collaboration Detailed Description
Missão na Guiné, Cabedu, 1963-1965 2018 Co-authorship with António José Ritto and José Colaço This book is a memorial and historical account of the Caçadores Company 555 (CCAC 555), which was deployed in the Cabedu subsector (Tombali Region, Guinea-Bissau). Norberto Gomes da Costa, who was part of the unit, contributes with his memory and knowledge, making the work an important testimony of military life and operations on the ground.
Articles and Contributions 2012 – Present Blog Luís Graça & Camaradas da Guiné He contributes frequently to the largest repository of memories of the Guinea War, providing details, archive information, and identifications of locations and comrades, demonstrating his role in the reconstruction of the collective memory of his Company and detachment.

  • Sources: His methodology is based on the exhaustive exploration of documentary collections, namely the Regional Periodical Press, which he catalogs and uses to contextualize major events of Portuguese History at the local level.
